Plugging into the Future: An Exploration of Electricity Consumption Patterns

Overview

Electricity is a vital driver of economic activity, and its consumption reflects both industrial productivity and household energy access. In India, agriculture accounts for one of the largest shares of electricity usage. Although electricity tariffs are relatively low, per-capita electricity consumption remains below global averages, indicating significant growth opportunities and infrastructure challenges.

The COVID-19 pandemic caused major changes in electricity demand across different sectors. Industrial and transportation electricity usage declined during lockdowns, while residential electricity consumption increased as people stayed at home.

This project analyzes electricity consumption patterns across Indian states from January 2019 to December 2020. The analysis covers the COVID-19 period and the nationwide lockdown (March–June 2020), providing insights into regional and national electricity usage trends.

Objectives

  • Study electricity consumption across Indian states.
  • Compare electricity usage before and during COVID-19.
  • Identify consumption trends in different sectors.
  • Visualize monthly and yearly electricity usage.
  • Support better energy planning and policy decisions.
